Meet the Founder

Most consultants talk about GCCs. Viraj has actually built one.

An IIT Bombay alumnus and engineer at heart — Viraj spent twenty years inside the machine. Not advising from the outside. Not reviewing decks in a boardroom. But building, scaling, and fixing things from the inside — across three continents, multiple Fortune 500 companies, and some of the most complex supply chain and capability challenges in the industry.

It started at Procter & Gamble, where he cut his teeth across procurement, contract manufacturing, planning, and logistics. He helped launch P&G's Planning Service Centres (GCC) in China and Singapore from scratch.

At General Mills, he took charge of the entire IMEA and India supply chain — launching 9 sites in 18 months and driving S&OP excellence across the region.

Then came the big one. Kraft Heinz handed him a blank page and a mandate: build Kraft Heinz's first-ever Global Capability Center. Not in Bangalore or Hyderabad — but in a tier-2 city. He built a centre with 1,000+ installed capacity across 20+ Centres of Excellence, achieving under 5% attrition against an industry average of 20%.
