The Context

GBS Vendor Relationships Drift.
Quietly, and Over Time.

When a GBS outsourcing contract is signed, both parties are focused. Commitments are clear, service levels are specific, headcount is agreed, and rates are negotiated carefully. Three years later, that same relationship often looks quite different — not through any single dramatic event, but through the slow accumulation of small divergences from what was originally agreed.

The challenge is structural. Finance teams can verify that invoices are paid — but rarely have the delivery depth to challenge what is billed. Procurement negotiated the contract, then moved to the next project. The GBS leadership team is close to the vendor relationship, making independent challenge difficult. The result is a gap — sometimes modest, sometimes significant — between what was contracted, what is billed, and what is actually delivered.

Most organisations only discover the full picture when a contract comes up for renewal, a new CFO asks pointed questions, or a leadership transition creates the moment for a clean look. The BEANZ ACCORD™ audit is designed to create that moment on your terms — with evidence, at a time of your choosing.

Situations That Typically Trigger an Audit

  • A major contract renewal is approaching and you want a clear picture of current standing before the negotiation begins
  • A new CFO, COO, or GBS Head has joined and wants an independent baseline — rather than inheriting assumptions from a prior regime
  • Unexplained cost increases have appeared in GBS spend without clear corresponding changes in scope or delivery
  • Business stakeholders have raised concerns about delivery quality, responsiveness, or staffing that have not been formally assessed
  • A prior internal attempt to review the relationship did not produce conclusive findings — too close to the vendor, or lacking the benchmarking data to challenge positions
  • The board or HQ has requested independent assurance on a material outsourcing arrangement

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions:
BEANZ ACCORD™ GBS Vendor Audit

What is a GBS vendor audit and who needs one?

A GBS vendor audit is an independent review of your outsourced GBS vendor relationships — examining whether vendors are delivering what your contracts specify, at the agreed rates, headcount, and service levels. It is designed for CFOs, COOs, and Procurement leaders who want an evidence-based view of the relationship, particularly ahead of a renewal, a leadership transition, or a board-level review. Any organisation with annual GBS outsourcing spend above USD 5M has material exposure that warrants periodic independent review.

How is BEANZ ACCORD™ different from an internal audit?

An internal audit team typically does not have close familiarity with GBS delivery specifics, and rarely has the market benchmarking data needed to challenge vendor positions on rates, headcount, or service levels. The BEANZ ACCORD™ audit is conducted by an independent practitioner with direct GBS operating experience at Fortune 500 scale — which allows substantive challenge of billing, headcount, and governance positions that an internal team is not typically positioned to make. Independence also means findings are not filtered through the relationship considerations that inevitably shape internal assessments.

Does the vendor need to be involved or aware of the audit?

No. The BEANZ ACCORD™ audit works entirely from documentation the client already holds — contracts, MSAs, SOWs, rate cards, invoices, governance records, headcount data, and SLA reports. Vendor participation is not required for the audit to proceed. You retain complete control over if, when, and how findings are shared with your vendor. Many clients choose to present findings directly in a renewal negotiation or governance forum.

What does the engagement cost?

BEANZ ACCORD™ is priced as a fixed fee, scoped at the outset of the engagement based on the number of vendors under review, the complexity of the contract structure, and the geographic scope of delivery. There are no success fees, no percentage-of-recovery arrangements, and no variable pricing. Fixed-fee pricing is a structural independence feature — it means there is no financial incentive to overstate findings, inflate the significance of issues, or pursue extended engagement beyond what is necessary. Please contact us for a scoping conversation and proposal.

How long does the engagement take, and how disruptive is it?

A standard BEANZ ACCORD™ engagement runs five weeks from mobilisation to executive readout. The primary demands on the client team are document collection in Week 1 (usually handled by a single point of contact) and stakeholder interviews in Weeks 2–3 (typically 45–60 minutes per stakeholder, scheduled at their convenience). There is no disruption to live vendor operations at any point. Most clients describe the engagement as significantly less demanding than a typical internal audit or procurement review.

What happens after the audit? Will you help us act on the findings?

The Action Roadmap deliverable is designed to be self-executing — every recommendation is mapped to a specific contractual lever or governance action already available to you, so no further advisory is required to begin acting on findings. For clients who want support in negotiation preparation, vendor conversations, or contract restructuring following the audit, Beanz Consulting can provide that as a separate follow-on engagement. There is no obligation, and it is structured as a distinct scope — not an extension of the ACCORD™ audit itself.
